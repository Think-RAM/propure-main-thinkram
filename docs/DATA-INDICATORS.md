# Propure - Data Indicators Reference

> **Purpose**: Complete reference for all data indicators used in property investment analysis. Each indicator includes definition, calculation method, data sources, and investment implications.

---

## Overview

The specific factors are both **quantitative and qualitative** and should be interpreted together to holistically assess capital growth prospects.

### Framework Structure

| Dimension | Categories |
|-----------|------------|
| **Geographic Layer** | National → City/Town → Suburb → Street → Property |
| **Timeframe** | Short-term (0-5 years) / Long-term (6-15 years) |
| **Investor Control** | Within control / Outside control |

---

## 1. Australia-Level (National) Indicators

### 1.1 Short-Term Indicators (0-5 Years)

#### Government Incentives

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Rebates, grants, tax offsets, or land-linked benefits offered by government |
| **Why It Matters** | Stimulates demand and investor confidence in targeted areas |
| **What to Look For** | Location-specific programs like land tax discounts, development bonuses, and homebuyer grants |
| **Formula** | Not applicable (policy driven or zoned) |
| **Sources** | [FirstHome.gov.au](https://www.firsthome.gov.au/), [ATO Incentives](https://www.ato.gov.au/), [QRO QLD](https://qro.qld.gov.au/property-concessions-grants/first-home-grant/), [Treasury](https://treasury.gov.au/policy-topics/housing/home-ownership-support), [FHSS Scheme](https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/super-for-individuals-and-families/super/withdrawing-and-using-your-super/early-access-to-super/first-home-super-saver-scheme), [Budget Housing](https://budget.gov.au/content/03-housing.htm) |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Attracts investors to specific regions or asset types through added financial leverage
- **Why**: Government benefits lower risk and increase ROI through subsidies, discounts, or tax advantages
- **If Not Considered**: Ignoring such benefits may result in choosing less competitive, high-risk markets with lower after-tax returns

---

#### Availability of Credit

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Bank lending standards and ease of obtaining finance |
| **Why It Matters** | Affects how easily buyers can access finance |
| **What to Look For** | Monthly lending indicator trends |
| **Formula** | Monitored via report PDFs |
| **Sources** | [RBA Lending Data](https://www.rba.gov.au/statistics/) |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Controls the flow of capital into the housing market
- **Why**: Tighter credit makes borrowing harder, reducing demand and lowering prices
- **If Not Considered**: Investors may misjudge market liquidity and overextend financially

---

#### Official Cash Rate

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | RBA-set interest rate that influences all lending rates |
| **Why It Matters** | Directly influences mortgage rates and borrowing costs |
| **What to Look For** | Historical and current cash rate values, RBA forward guidance |
| **Formula** | Set by RBA (refer monthly table) |
| **Sources** | [RBA Cash Rate](https://www.rba.gov.au/statistics/cash-rate/) |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Changes the cost of borrowing for buyers and investors
- **Why**: Higher rates reduce affordability and investor return
- **If Not Considered**: Investing during rate hikes can erode yields and lower resale value

---

#### Consumer Confidence

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Buyer optimism and willingness to make major purchases |
| **Why It Matters** | High confidence drives purchasing activity |
| **What to Look For** | Confidence index trends (monthly) |
| **Formula** | Index published monthly (tracked) |
| **Sources** | [Westpac-Melbourne Consumer Sentiment Index](https://www.westpac.com.au/news/mind-over-money/2023/10/australian-consumer-sentiment/) |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Reflects general sentiment and likelihood of purchase action
- **Why**: Confidence can push buyers to act even in less-than-ideal conditions
- **If Not Considered**: Investors may misread false positives or ignore caution signs

---

#### GDP Growth

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | National economic growth rate |
| **Why It Matters** | Strong GDP supports long-term price growth and employment |
| **What to Look For** | Quarterly GDP updates |
| **Formula** | `(GDP Current - GDP Previous) / GDP Previous × 100` |
| **Sources** | [ABS National Accounts](https://www.abs.gov.au/statistics/economy/national-accounts) |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Drives macro stability, income growth, and housing demand
- **Why**: Economic strength encourages job security and property ownership
- **If Not Considered**: Investment in weak economic phases risks poor appreciation

---

#### Employment Levels

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Percentage of people employed in the labour force |
| **Why It Matters** | Affects household income and buyer activity |
| **What to Look For** | Unemployment rate, job participation rate |
| **Formula** | `(Employed People / Labour Force) × 100` |
| **Sources** | [ABS Labour Force](https://www.abs.gov.au/statistics/labour/employment-and-unemployment/labour-force-australia) |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Indicates financial ability to purchase and hold property
- **Why**: Employment drives borrowing power and consistent cash flow
- **If Not Considered**: Investors risk vacancies and arrears in high-unemployment areas

#### Political Stability

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Government predictability and policy consistency |
| **Why It Matters** | Encourages long-term foreign and local investment |
| **What to Look For** | Federal policy directions, long-term reforms |

- **If Not Considered**: Risk of sudden policy changes or unrest derailing long-term returns

## 2. City/Town-Level Indicators

### 2.1 Short-Term Indicators (0-5 Years)

#### Job Infrastructure

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Commercial zones, employment hubs, business districts |
| **Why It Matters** | Generates demand for rentals and sales |
| **What to Look For** | Local infrastructure plans, zoning updates |
| **Formula** | Not applicable (qualitative observation) |
| **Sources** | [Infrastructure Australia](https://www.infrastructureaustralia.gov.au/), Google Maps |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Attracts working population and raises rental demand
- **Why**: Proximity to jobs increases livability and cash flow opportunities
- **If Not Considered**: Investing far from employment centers risks low tenant appeal and price stagnation

---

#### Building Approvals

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Pipeline of residential developments approved for construction |
| **Why It Matters** | Indicates upcoming supply that may affect prices |
| **What to Look For** | Monthly approvals by city/region |
| **Formula** | `Total Approved Units / Time Period` |
| **Sources** | [ABS Building Approvals](https://www.abs.gov.au/statistics/industry/building-and-construction/building-approvals-australia) |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Warns of possible oversupply or development boom
- **Why**: High approvals = more supply, which can suppress price growth
- **If Not Considered**: Buying during supply spikes can reduce rental yields and slow capital growth

---

#### Job Advertisements

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Volume of hiring demand in the region |
| **Why It Matters** | Reflects short-term local economic momentum |
| **What to Look For** | Job ads by occupation/region |
| **Formula** | Count listings per region or occupation |
| **Sources** | [Jobs & Skills Australia](https://www.jobsandskills.gov.au/data/occupation-and-industry-profiles), [SEEK](https://www.seek.com.au/), [CareerOne](https://www.careerone.com.au/) |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Signals business expansion and population inflow
- **Why**: High job ads = more movers, renters, and buyers
- **If Not Considered**: Missing job growth may cause misjudged demand and overinvestment

---

### 2.2 Long-Term Indicators (6-15 Years)

#### Industry Diversity

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Resilience of local economy across multiple sectors |
| **Why It Matters** | Diversified economy = lower volatility |
| **What to Look For** | Percentage of jobs across different industries |
| **Formula** | `Jobs per Industry / Total Jobs × 100` |
| **Sources** | [ABS Census Employment](https://www.abs.gov.au/census/find-census-data) |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Makes a region more shock-proof to economic downturns
- **Why**: Diverse economies keep housing demand stable across sectors
- **If Not Considered**: Risk of collapse if one dominant industry declines (e.g., mining towns)

---

#### Housing Affordability

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Price-to-income ratio for the region |
| **Why It Matters** | Determines buying power and market sustainability |
| **What to Look For** | Regional income vs price trends |
| **Formula** | `Median Property Price / Median Income` |
| **Sources** | ABS, Domain, REA |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Gauges long-term demand sustainability
- **Why**: Affordable areas attract steady buyer and investor interest
- **If Not Considered**: Overpaying in overvalued zones can delay breakeven or result in lower ROI

## 3. Suburb-Level Indicators

### 3.1 Short-Term Indicators (0-5 Years)

#### Vacancy Rate

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Percentage of rental properties currently unoccupied |
| **Why It Matters** | Low vacancy = strong rental demand |
| **What to Look For** | Vacancies by suburb |
| **Formula** | `(Vacant / Total Rentals) × 100` |
| **Sources** | [Domain Rental Report](https://www.domain.com.au/research/rental-report/), Domain and RE listings against total dwellings |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Low vacancies mean high tenant demand
- **Why**: Signals strong rental income potential and less downtime
- **If Not Considered**: Overestimating rental potential can lead to longer vacancies and lower ROI

---

#### Auction Clearance Rate

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Percentage of auctions resulting in sales |
| **Why It Matters** | High clearance = strong buyer competition |
| **What to Look For** | Weekly suburb auction stats |
| **Formula** | `(Sold Auctions / Total Auctions) × 100` |
| **Sources** | [Domain Auctions](https://www.domain.com.au/auction-results/), REA Auctions |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Reflects market demand and urgency
- **Why**: High clearance suggests buyers are willing to pay premium or bid fast
- **If Not Considered**: Can lead to poor timing decisions like buying during low-demand cycles

---

#### Days on Market

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Average time from listing to sale |
| **Why It Matters** | Shorter = higher demand |
| **What to Look For** | Track listing & sale dates |
| **Formula** | `Sale Date - List Date` (manual scrape) |
| **Sources** | Domain, REA Listings |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Measures buyer interest and sales speed
- **Why**: Quicker sales indicate hot markets attractive to investors
- **If Not Considered**: May enter sluggish markets with delayed returns

---

#### Vendor Discounting

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Difference between asking price and final sale price |
| **Why It Matters** | Small discount = hot market with seller power |
| **What to Look For** | Asking vs sold prices |
| **Formula** | `(List Price - Sale Price) / List Price × 100` |
| **Sources** | Domain, REA Insights |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Shows buyer competition levels
- **Why**: Low discounts mean sellers hold power = investor upside
- **If Not Considered**: Can misjudge true market value and overpay

---

#### Gross Yield

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Annual rental income as percentage of property price |
| **Why It Matters** | Indicates cash return potential |
| **What to Look For** | Rent/Price comparison by suburb |
| **Formula** | `(Annual Rent / Property Price) × 100` |
| **Sources** | Domain Rental Yield |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Measures rental return on investment
- **Why**: Higher yields indicate better cash flow in that suburb
- **If Not Considered**: Could buy into low-income areas with negative cash flow risk

---

#### Stock on Market

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Number of active listings in the suburb |
| **Why It Matters** | Low stock = high demand pressure |
| **What to Look For** | Active listings count |
| **Formula** | `Total Listings per 30 days` |
| **Sources** | [Domain Listings](https://www.domain.com.au/) |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Tracks supply pressure in micro-markets
- **Why**: Less stock often means upward price pressure
- **If Not Considered**: Misreading saturation can lead to investing in a falling market

---

#### Demand-Supply Ratio

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Ratio of buyer interest to available properties |
| **Why It Matters** | High ratio = growth potential |
| **What to Look For** | Compare listings vs views/searches |
| **Formula** | `Searches / Listings` (manual ratio) |
| **Sources** | REA Insights |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Shows imbalance in buyer vs property volume
- **Why**: High DSR = faster sales, higher competition
- **If Not Considered**: Can lead to investing in low-performing suburbs with minimal growth

---

#### Rental Growth

| **Why It Matters** | Indicates investor appeal and income trajectory |

| **Sources** | Domain Rent Report, Historical Data |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Reveals rent trend trajectory

- **If Not Considered**: Investors may buy into stagnant or declining rental areas

## 4. Additional Risk & Opportunity Indicators

These indicators apply across levels and provide additional risk assessment and opportunity identification.

### Price Volatility Index

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Measures suburb-level price fluctuations |
| **Why It Matters** | Helps assess risk and stability |
| **What to Look For** | Monthly price standard deviation |
| **Formula** | `Std Dev / Average Price × 100` |
| **Sources** | [Domain Research](https://www.domain.com.au/research/) or manual calculation from listings |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Captures short-term price stability
- **Why**: Low volatility = safer asset class; high = potential for loss or windfall
- **If Not Considered**: Can misjudge timing and expose capital to undue risk

---

### Population Growth (Suburb/LGA)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Rate of population increase in the area |
| **Why It Matters** | Predicts future buyer/renter interest |
| **What to Look For** | 5-year population change |
| **Formula** | `(New Pop - Old Pop) / Old Pop × 100` |
| **Sources** | ABS Regional Population |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Informs if long-term demand will rise
- **Why**: Higher population creates consistent buyer and rental demand
- **If Not Considered**: Risk of investing in declining areas with minimal appreciation potential

---

### Owner-Occupier vs Investor Ratio

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Proportion of properties owned by investors vs owner-occupiers |
| **Why It Matters** | Housing stability indicator |
| **What to Look For** | Percentage of investment-owned dwellings |
| **Formula** | `Investor-owned / Total Properties × 100` |
| **Sources** | [ABS Tenure Type](https://www.abs.gov.au/census/find-census-data) |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Influences volatility and tenant mix
- **Why**: High investor ratio = higher turnover and price fluctuation
- **If Not Considered**: Could lead to unpredictable yield swings and resale difficulties

---

### Infrastructure Pipeline Score

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Forecasts new value drivers

- **If Not Considered**: Investors miss pre-boom buying windows and pay peak prices later

---

### Mortgage Stress Level

|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Percentage of households paying >30% of income on mortgage |
| **Why It Matters** | Indicates future price pressure in downturn |
| **What to Look For** | Percentage of stressed households |
| **Formula** | Tracked via report (or custom survey) |
| **Sources** | Digital Finance Analytics, surveys |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Measures fragility in property holding power
- **Why**: High stress areas risk higher defaults during downturns
- **If Not Considered**: Can enter markets prone to forced sales or price collapses

---

### Time to Rent vs Sell

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What It Means** | Comparison of days on market for rentals vs sales |
| **Why It Matters** | Demand clarity per suburb |
| **What to Look For** | Compare days on market for sale vs rent |
| **Formula** | `Avg Rent Days vs Avg Sell Days` |
| **Sources** | [Domain Listings](https://www.domain.com.au/) |

**Investment Effect**:
- **What**: Helps investors understand market liquidity
- **Why**: Rental lag can indicate leasing weakness despite sales performance
- **If Not Considered**: Could suffer from unoccupied properties post-purchase
